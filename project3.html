Maternal and Child Health Monitoring in LMICs

Machine learning for granular, scalable monitoring of maternal and child health indicators using satellite imagery and geotagged data in low- and middle-income countries.

Project Overview

This project aims to leverage machine learning and remote sensing to estimate key maternal and child health (MCH) indicators at the village and neighborhood level in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs). By analyzing satellite imagery and geotagged data, the workflow enables accurate, scalable, and cost-effective monitoring of MCH status, supporting targeted interventions and policy planning.

Workflow & Methodology
1. Data Acquisition & Preprocessing

Publicly available satellite imagery and geotagged datasets were collected for selected LMIC regions. Data preprocessing included cleaning, normalization, and the extraction of relevant features from both spatial and tabular sources. Training labels were preprocessed to align with the spatial resolution of the satellite data.

2. Feature Engineering & Selection

Geospatial and environmental features were engineered from satellite imagery, including vegetation indices, built-up area metrics, and proximity to health infrastructure. Feature selection was performed using tree-based methods to identify the most predictive variables for each MCH indicator.

3. Model Development

Multiple machine learning models were trained and evaluated, including KNN Regressor, XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost, as well as single-task and multi-task learning approaches. Models were optimized for accuracy and generalizability, with cross-validation to ensure robustness.

4. Indicator Estimation & Validation

The best-performing models were used to estimate MCH indicators at village and neighborhood level. Results were validated against available ground truth data and compared across different regions to assess model transferability and reliability.

Key Features
  • Granular, scalable estimation of maternal and child health indicators
  • Integration of satellite imagery and geotagged data
  • Multiple machine learning models: KNN, XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost, single/multi-task learning
  • Automated feature selection and label preprocessing
  • Village- and neighborhood-level outputs for targeted intervention
